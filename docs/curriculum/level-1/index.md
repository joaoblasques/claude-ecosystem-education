# Level 1 — The Claude Apps

**Seven days. Zero code. One shipped mini-project per day.**

Level 1 teaches you the Claude ecosystem from the consumer side: claude.ai in the browser, the desktop app, the Chrome extension, and the Excel add-in. You will not open a terminal, write a line of code, or touch an API key. By the end of the week you will have built an interactive app, a working Skill, a connected workspace, and an automation loop that survives past this course.

## Who this is for

- You use Claude (or any chatbot) mostly for Q&A and want to get real work out of it.
- You are not a developer — or you are, but you want to master the app layer before Level 2's tooling.
- You can commit roughly 45–90 minutes a day for a week.

You need a Claude account. Most days work on the free tier; a paid plan unlocks the Chrome extension and heavier usage. Where a specific plan is required, the day page says so.

## The learn-in-public rule

Every day ends with a **Build** (something concrete you ship) and a **Log it** entry (a short public post about what you built, what broke, and what surprised you). Post the log anywhere people can see it — LinkedIn, X, a blog, a team channel. The log is not optional garnish; writing it is how the day's material sticks, and the trail of posts becomes your proof of work.

## The 7 days

| Day | Topic | What you ship |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| [Day 1](day-1.md) | Meet the ecosystem — claude.ai done right | A configured Project + a personal "25 modes" scorecard |
| [Day 2](day-2.md) | Artifacts — build your first interactive app | A working calculator or tool you can share by link |
| [Day 3](day-3.md) | Skills — teach Claude your standards | One tested Skill for your most-repeated task |
| [Day 4](day-4.md) | Connectors & MCP — plug in your real data | Gmail/Calendar/Drive connected, with a deliberate permission map |
| [Day 5](day-5.md) | Agentic Claude — Chrome, Excel, Cowork | One browser or spreadsheet workflow automated end to end |
| [Day 6](day-6.md) | Thinking & learning with Claude | A learning session log + a persistent-memory folder |
| [Day 7](day-7.md) | Capstone — stop prompting, build loops | Your first installed loop, run three times |

## How the days build on each other

Days 1–3 are about **capability**: what the apps can do and how to configure them so quality is high on the first try. Day 4 adds **your data**. Day 5 adds **action** — Claude doing things, not just saying things. Day 6 turns Claude on **yourself** as a thinking and learning tool. Day 7 assembles everything into a **loop**: a reusable system with memory, a trigger, and verification, which is the whole point of the week.

Do the days in order. Each Build assumes the previous ones exist.

!!! tip "Pace"
    If a day runs long, ship the Build and skip the stretch goals. A shipped small thing beats an unshipped complete thing — that principle repeats all week.

## Moving to Level 2

Level 2 leaves the apps and enters the developer tooling: Claude Code in the terminal, CLAUDE.md configuration, hooks, subagents, and the Agent SDK. You are ready for it when:

1. All seven Builds are shipped and logged.
2. Your Day 7 loop has run at least three times on real work.
3. You can explain, in one paragraph each, what a Project, a Skill, a Connector, and a loop are — without looking them up.

That last check matters: Level 2 reuses every one of those concepts, just closer to the metal.
