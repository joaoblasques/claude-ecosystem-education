# 2026-07-08 — Day 0: the program builds itself

**Shipped:** this site — curriculum skeleton (3 levels, 7 days + 8 modules + 4 builder
projects), the learn-in-public mechanics, and the two maintenance loops
(`/log`, `/curriculum-review`).

## What I did

- Deep-researched the source material: a knowledge-base query over ~150 pages of
  collected Claude-ecosystem notes, a 20-post sweep of the
  [AI in public](https://www.aiinpublic.com) newsletter's Claude-mastery cluster
  (the "learn Claude in 7 days" roadmap that seeded this), and a web research pass on
  curriculum precedent (learn-in-public programs like #100DaysOfCode) and naming
  constraints.
- Designed the three-level structure: the newsletter posts map cleanly onto a no-code
  apps week (Level 1); the deeper harness material (context economy, CLAUDE.md,
  skills, MCP, subagents) becomes Level 2; contribution projects become Level 3.
- Scaffolded the site (MkDocs Material), the curriculum pages, and this log.

## What broke / surprised me

- All 19 roadmap links from the original LinkedIn post 301-redirect: the newsletter
  rebranded from humzakhalid.substack.com to aiinpublic.com — one link was dead until
  resolved against the new domain's sitemap. Lesson for the curriculum-review loop:
  **links rot fast in this space; check them weekly.**
- Several source posts are partially paywalled. Forced a good content rule: describe
  and credit, never reproduce — the curriculum teaches the concepts, the sources keep
  their prompts.
- The "7-day roadmap" turned out not to be a numbered series at all — just a cluster
  of standalone posts. The 7-day arc here is original sequencing.
